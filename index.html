About Me.

Publications

Scanning gate microscopy in graphene nanostructures
X.-Z. Chen, Guillaume Weick, Dietmar Weinmann, Rodolfo A. Jalabert
Phys. Rev. B 107, 085420

We examines how scanning gate microscopy (SGM) probes affect the conductance of graphene nanoribbons and nanoconstrictions. Using a noninvasive scattering approach, we calculate first- and second-order conductance corrections due to the tip potential, finding that the second-order term dominates in conductance plateaus of armchair graphene strips, following a universal scaling law. For strong tip potentials, resonances emerge from trapped states beneath the tip. Notably, the zero-transverse-energy mode in metallic armchair nanoribbons remains insensitive to long-range probe fields. In nanoconstrictions, SGM reveals how conductance quantization breaks down: first-order corrections dominate at low tip strength, while second-order effects prevail near faint plateaus. The most significant tip impact occurs centrally near edges, correlating with local density of states. Zigzag-edged structures behave similarly to armchair ones, except when tip-induced intervalley coupling disrupts chiral edge states.
